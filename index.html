About

Xiaomin (Billy) Zhong is a health data epidemiologist in the Nuffield Department of Population Health at the University of Oxford, based in the Applied Health Research Unit. His research uses large national health datasets to inform public health policy and clinical care.

He works across cardiovascular disease, infection, and cancer, with a focus on hospital-acquired venous thromboembolism, antibiotic use and sepsis, and long-term trends in mortality and hospital care. He holds a PhD in Health Informatics and an MSc in Health Data Science from the University of Manchester, and a BSc from Peking University. He also teaches on the Health Data Science MSc at Manchester and supervises postgraduate research projects at Oxford Population Health.

Research themes

01

Venous thromboembolism prevention

Hospital-acquired blood clots, how they are prevented, and the safety trade-off with major bleeding, studied across national hospital records.

02

Infection, antibiotics and sepsis

Antibiotic prescribing, bloodstream infection, and sepsis outcomes before, during, and after the COVID-19 pandemic.

03

Cancer and mortality trends

Long-term trends in cancer mortality and end-of-life care, drawing on multiple-cause death records linked to hospital data.

04

Methods with national health data

Analysing Hospital Episode Statistics and OpenSAFELY records at population scale to answer questions that matter for policy and clinical care.
